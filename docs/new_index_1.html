Research Focus

The ADAP Framework

Adaptive Digital Academic Practice examines how researchers and students navigate digital tools, accessibility needs, and institutional constraints as epistemic resources rather than barriers. This framework bridges necessity entrepreneurship theory with academic knowledge production, positioning adaptive strategies as generative rather than compensatory.

Key Research Questions

  • How do constraints shape epistemic practices in academic work?
  • What role do adaptive technologies play in knowledge production?
  • How can institutional structures better support diverse learning and working styles?
  • What can entrepreneurship theory teach us about academic innovation under constraint?

Research & Writing

Monograph

Adaptive Digital Academic Practice: Disability, Structural Constraint, and Epistemic Entrepreneurship in Graduate Research
Professional Doctorate Portfolio | 2024–2025

The central work applying necessity entrepreneurship theory to academic knowledge production. Examines how structural constraints (institutional design, accessibility gaps, technological limitations) shape adaptive practices that become epistemic resources. Positions disability and constraint not as deficits but as sites of innovative problem-solving and knowledge creation.

Status: Ready for submission to SSRN, SocArXiv, EdArXiv, and PPPR.

Companion Papers

Five peer-reviewed papers that expand on specific dimensions of the ADAP framework:

Paper 1: Necessity Entrepreneurship in Academic Knowledge Production
Journal Article | ~8,000 words

Applies necessity entrepreneurship theory directly to graduate research and academic knowledge production. Explores how resource scarcity and structural constraints drive innovation in research methodologies and dissemination strategies.

Paper 2: Digital Tools as Epistemic Scaffolding in Distributed Academic Work
Journal Article | ~8,000 words

Examines how digital platforms, AI-assisted research tools, and accessible technologies shape epistemic practices. Challenges the "digital distraction" narrative by positioning technological mediation as generative for certain knowledge producers.

Paper 3: Accessibility as Institutional Design: Lessons from Neurodivergent Scholars
Journal Article | ~8,000 words

Uses neurodiversity as a case study for institutional accessibility. Argues that accommodations benefiting neurodivergent scholars create systems that enhance cognitive flexibility for all researchers.

Paper 4: Constraint-Driven Entrepreneurship in Educational Technology
Journal Article | ~8,000 words

Applies the ADAP framework to EdTech product development. Examines how resource constraints and market gaps drive innovation in building SaaS platforms for underserved educational segments (homeschooling, microschools, co-ops).

Paper 5: Epistemic Justice and Distributed Knowledge in Academic Communities
Journal Article | ~8,000 words

Draws on epistemic justice theory to explore how constraint-driven adaptive practices create new forms of knowledge sharing, mentorship, and collaborative problem-solving in academic networks.

Publication Strategy: The monograph and five papers will be submitted to multiple preprint servers (SSRN, SocArXiv, EdArXiv) and PPPR to establish a citable preprint record. This approach prioritizes immediate scholarly visibility, bypasses journal gatekeeping delays, and strengthens applications to doctoral programs that recognize preprint scholarship.
